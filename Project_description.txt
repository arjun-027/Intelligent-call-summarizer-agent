Format Rules
Caller line: Include relationship (policyholder, third party representative, solicitor, family member, etc.) and call direction (inbound/outbound)
Subject: Single line, concise description
Executive Summary: Opening paragraph plus bullet points for key details
Next Steps: Always include company action and "Other" (use "None" if no action required)
Conditional sections: Only include if that topic was actually discussed on the call
Character limit: Total summary must be ≤ 1,500 characters
What Makes a Good Call Summary?
Every generated summary must pass these five quality checks. Use these questions as a pass/fail checklist when evaluating your outputs:

Can I identify the customer's issue/query, and what actions were taken?
The summary clearly captures why the call happened and what was done about it. A reader shouldn't have to guess at the core purpose of the conversation.
Are critical facts (amounts, dates, names, etc.) accurate?
Any specific details extracted from the transcript must be correct. The summary should not invent, misstate, or omit important factual information.
Would another customer agent understand what happened and/or what to do next?
The summary provides enough context for handoff. Someone picking up this case should be able to continue without re-listening to the call.
Is it professional enough that it could be shared with the customer?
The tone and language are appropriate for external communication. No internal jargon, casual language, or potentially embarrassing content.
Is the character count within the field limit (≤ 1,500 characters)?
The summary must be less than or equal to 1,500 characters to fit within downstream system constraints.
Common Summarisation Issues
Based on real production issues, watch out for:

Wrong caller identification
Labelling a third-party solicitor as "customer", or missing that the caller is the policyholder's father rather than the policyholder
Wrong company/party names
Getting the caller's company name wrong, or confusing which company insures whom
Hallucinated confirmations
Stating "customer confirmed bank details" when they didn't, or claiming a waiver was confirmed when it wasn't mentioned
Misidentified facts
Wrong reference numbers, incorrect email addresses, misspelled names
Missing critical details
Omitting callback phone numbers, IBANs, email addresses, or settlement waiver confirmations
Irrelevant sections
Including "Liability Summary: None" when liability wasn't discussed at all
Redundant information
Stating the same fact twice in different sections
Misunderstood relationships
Confusing who insures whom, or which party owns the dog/vehicle/property
